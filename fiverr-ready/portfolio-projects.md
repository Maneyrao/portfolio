## 01. Club Amsterdam - Custom Booking System

Industry: Beauty, wellness, local services or business services.

Suggested attachment:

- `/Users/tmaneyro22/Desktop/Proyectos/Portfolio/public/images/work/club-amsterdam-public-agenda.png`

Description:

Club Amsterdam is a barber shop with a carefully built brand identity: personal, premium and detailed. Their previous booking flow did not match that experience. It felt generic and did not help customers feel the same level of care before arriving at the shop.

I worked on a custom booking system designed around the brand, the client journey and the way the business operates. The goal was to make reservations clearer, more comfortable and more aligned with the premium experience Club Amsterdam wanted to offer.

The result was a responsive booking experience with a stronger visual identity, a smoother flow for customers and a digital experience that feels more connected to the real business.

## 02. Club Amsterdam - Admin Dashboard Demo

Industry: Business services, operations, local services.

Suggested attachment:

- `/Users/tmaneyro22/Desktop/Proyectos/Portfolio/public/images/work/club-amsterdam-admin-agenda.png`

Description:

This project shows the admin dashboard created for Club Amsterdam. The goal was to give the owners a clearer way to manage appointments, clients, services and daily availability from one internal panel.

Instead of depending on scattered messages, spreadsheets or generic tools, the dashboard centralizes the daily operation and makes the booking system easier to control. The interface follows the same visual identity as the public booking experience, so the internal tool also feels custom, professional and aligned with the brand.

It is a good example of how a small business can use a custom dashboard to reduce manual work and improve operational clarity.

## 05. De Fazio Propiedades - Real Estate Redesign

Industry: Real estate.

Suggested attachment:

- `/Users/tmaneyro22/Desktop/Proyectos/Portfolio/public/images/work/de-fazio-hero.png`

Description:

De Fazio had an institutional website originally created in 2017. It no longer reflected the current level of the real estate business or the standards users expect from a modern website.
